Tim's Spectrum Games

Lovingly crafted in my teenage years on a black and white monitor with a single ZX Spectrum, a rubbish tape recorder, and a set of C15 tapes. Graphics were done in Melbourne Draw. I've forgotten the name of the excellent assembler I used; I'll dig it out later.

You can play the games directly online from the links below.

Master and Servant: This was my last, best, game. It wasn't published, howver - the industry had moved past the ability of a 17-year-old with a tape deck and Melbourne Draw.

Jock and the Time RingsThis was commercially published by Atlantis Software, a budget publisher, and did OK. Reviews weren't great, and I really needed to understand acceleration and multi-pixel moves, but it's still a pretty fun game. My friends at Uni spent hours playing it in our lounge in an early example of multi-screening.
